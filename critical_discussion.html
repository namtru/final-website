Social Implications

DeepFake technology can undermine trust in media, promote disinformation, and lead to personal and societal harm. It highlights significant challenges in ensuring ethical use of emerging AI technologies.

Ethical Perspectives

Analyzing DeepFake technology through various ethical lenses reveals contrasting views. A deontological approach emphasizes its inherent risks, while a utilitarian perspective considers potential benefits in entertainment and education.

Recommendations

To minimize harm, governments and organizations should enforce regulations, develop detection tools, and educate the public on identifying fake media.
